## SLIDE 11: The Contribution Process

**Title:** Simple, Structured, Safe

**Content:**
**How it works:**

1. **You spot something** to add or correct
2. **Click "Suggest Edit"** or "Add..." button
3. **Fill out the form** with your changes
4. **Add a note** explaining your source/reasoning
5. **Submit** → goes to Editor review queue
6. **Editor reviews** → approves or requests changes
7. **Goes live** on the site immediately upon approval

**Every edit is tracked. Nothing is lost. Quality is maintained.**

## PRESENTATION TIPS

### Timing
- **Total presentation:** 45 minutes
  - Intro slides: 10 min
  - Live demo: 20 min
  - Ask & closing: 5 min
  - Q&A: 10 min

### Demo Preparation
**CRITICAL: Pre-stage everything**
1. Have 2 browsers open:
   - Browser 1: Contributor account (for submitting edits)
   - Browser 2: Editor account (for showing approval)
2. Pre-select specific synagogues and leaders for demo
3. Write down the exact edits you'll make during demo
4. Test the entire demo flow the night before
5. Have backup screenshots in case live demo fails

### What to Have Ready
- [ ] Demo script with specific URLs and edits written out
- [ ] Two browser windows/profiles logged in to different accounts
- [ ] Backup screenshots of every demo step
- [ ] Handout with site URL and your contact email
- [ ] List of specific examples from Philadelphia Jewish history
- [ ] Talking points about original data sources (with names)

### Potential Questions to Prepare For
1. "What if I make a mistake?" → Editors review everything, nothing goes live without approval
2. "Can I delete something I submitted?" → Yes, contact us or wait for Editor review
3. "What if two people submit conflicting information?" → Editors review both and make determination based on sources
4. "How do you handle controversial information?" → Editors discuss, seek sources, make informed decisions
5. "Can I export data for my own research?" → Not currently, but we can discuss specific needs
6. "What about living people?" → We focus on historical figures; living clergy are included but with limited detail
7. "How do I cite sources?" → Source field in every contribution form; we encourage documentation
8. "What if I disagree with an Editor's decision?" → Contact admin, discuss, we're open to reconsideration with new information

### Key Messages to Repeat
1. **Your knowledge matters** - you know things not in any database
2. **Safe to contribute** - nothing goes live without review
3. **Start small** - even one edit helps
4. **Community effort** - we're building this together
5. **Heritage preservation** - this is for future generations
